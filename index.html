About BYU Football

Brigham Young University football has produced some of the most innovative offenses and decorated players in college football history. Under legendary coach LaVell Edwards, BYU became synonymous with the passing game long before it was mainstream.

Today, competing in the Big 12 Conference, the Cougars continue to build on that legacy — sending players to the NFL, winning bowl games, and inspiring a passionate fanbase that spans the globe.

Did You Know?

Fun Facts About BYU Football & BYU

  1. BYU won the 1984 National Championship

    The Cougars finished 13-0, the first team ever to complete an undefeated season and be crowned national champions. Key contributors that year included:

    • QB Robbie Bosco, who threw for 3,875 yards despite playing injured
    • Coach LaVell Edwards, who masterminded the attack
    • A Holiday Bowl victory over Michigan to seal the title
  2. Ty Detmer won the 1990 Heisman Trophy

    BYU's only Heisman winner threw for over 5,000 yards and 41 TDs in a single season. His records at the time were staggering:

    • 5,188 passing yards — an NCAA record in 1990
    • 41 touchdown passes in one season
    • 15,031 career passing yards at BYU — a program record
  3. BYU has produced multiple NFL Hall of Famers

    The Cougars have sent elite talent to the NFL for decades. Notable alumni include:

    • Steve Young — Pro Football Hall of Fame, 3× Super Bowl champion
    • Jim McMahon — led the 1985 Bears to a Super Bowl XX title
    • Zach Wilson — 2nd overall pick in the 2021 NFL Draft
  4. LaVell Edwards revolutionized college football

    Before spread offenses were everywhere, LaVell built passing attacks that changed the sport. His legacy lives on:

    • 257 career wins as BYU head coach (1972–2000)
    • The stadium was renamed in his honor in 2000
    • Inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame in 2004
  5. BYU joined the Big 12 Conference in 2023

    After years as an independent, BYU took a major step up in competition. Highlights so far:

    • First Big 12 season brought national attention back to Provo
    • LaVell Edwards Stadium holds roughly 63,000 fans
    • The "Holy War" rivalry vs. Utah is one of the most heated in the West
  6. Interesting facts about BYU the university

    Beyond football, BYU itself is a remarkable institution:

    • Owned and operated by The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints
    • Consistently ranked among the top universities for return on investment
    • Students come from all 50 states and over 100 countries worldwide
    • Known for its honor code, academic rigor, and strong business programs
